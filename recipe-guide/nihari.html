nihari

Description

Nihari is a stew originating in Lucknow, the capital of 18th-century Awadh under the Mughal Empire in the Indian subcontinent. It consists of slow-cooked meat, mainly a shank cut of beef, lamb and mutton, or goat meat, as well as chicken and bone marrow. It is flavoured with long pepper, a relative of black pepper.

Ingredients

  • Beef or lamb shank, bone-in
  • Onions, thinly sliced
  • Garlic, minced
  • Ginger, minced
  • Nihari masala (or a combination of cumin, coriander, black pepper, cloves, and cardamom)
  • Turmeric powder
  • Red chili powder
  • Salt
  • Oil or ghee
  • Wheat flour (optional, for thickening)
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped (for garnish)
  • Fresh lemon wedges (for serving)
  • Ginger slices (for garnish)
  • Green chilies, sliced (for garnish)

Recipe

  1. Heat oil or ghee in a large pot over medium heat.
  2. Add thinly sliced onions and cook until they are golden brown.
  3. Add minced garlic and ginger to the pot and saut√© for a few minutes until fragrant.
  4. Add the beef or lamb shank pieces to the pot and cook until they are browned on all sides.
  5. Add Nihari masala, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Stir well to coat the meat with the spices.
  6. Pour in enough water to cover the meat. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to low.
  7. Cover the pot and let it simmer for several hours, or until the meat is tender and falling off the bone. You can also cook it in a slow cooker or pressure cooker if preferred.
  8. If using, mix wheat flour with a little water to make a paste and stir it into the pot to thicken the gravy.
  9. Once the meat is tender and the gravy has thickened, check the seasoning and adjust if necessary.
  10. Garnish with chopped cilantro, fresh lemon wedges, ginger slices, and sliced green chilies before serving.
  11. Serve hot with naan or steamed rice.
